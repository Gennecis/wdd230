Know the city

Historical Significance: Abeokuta, meaning "under the rock" in Yoruba, was founded in 1830. It served as a refuge from slave hunters and was established by people escaping from the Oyo Empire. The city's rocky landscape provided natural fortification.

Geography: The city is situated on the east bank of the Ogun River and is surrounded by large granite rocks, including the famous Olumo Rock, which has historical and cultural significance.

Economy: Abeokuta is a significant trade center for agricultural products like rice, yams, cassava, maize, cotton, and palm oil. It also has industries such as fruit canning, plastics, breweries, sawmills, and an aluminum products factory.

Cultural Heritage: The city is known for its rich Yoruba culture, with traditional festivals, arts, and crafts, including the famous Adire textile (tie and dye) industry.

Landmarks: Key landmarks include Olumo Rock, which has ancient caves and shrines, the Centenary Hall, and the Ake Palace, the traditional home of the Alake, the paramount ruler of Egbaland.

Education: Abeokuta hosts several educational institutions, including the Federal University of Agriculture and several secondary and primary schools.

Population: As of recent estimates, the city's population is about 600,000, with a mix of various ethnic groups, though predominantly Yoruba.

Tourist Attractions

  • Olumo Rock: This iconic natural fortress is a must-visit. It offers stunning views of the city and has historical significance as a refuge for the Egba people during wars.
  • Ake Palace:: A historical site that provides a glimpse into the traditional royalty of the Egba people.
  • Adire Market: Known for its vibrant display of Adire (tie-dyed) fabrics, this market is a great place to experience local crafts and culture.
  • Olusegun Obasanjo Presidential Library: This library and museum offer insights into the life and times of Nigeriaâ€™s former president, Olusegun Obasanjo.
  • Centenary Hall: Built to commemorate 100 years of peace in Abeokuta, this hall is an architectural marvel and a significant historical site.
  • OOPL Wildlife Park: Located within the Olusegun Obasanjo Presidential Library complex, this park is home to various wildlife species and offers a family-friendly environment.
  • Itoku Market: Another bustling market known for its pottery and crafts, providing a unique shopping experience.

Local Businesses

  • Dartom Chemicals Enterprise: Deals in household chemicals like laundry agents, liquid soap, and air.
